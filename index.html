Arctic Monkeys

The Arctic Monkeys are a British rock band formed in Sheffield, England in 2002. The band consists of Alex Turner (lead vocals, guitar, piano), Jamie Cook (guitar, keyboards), Nick O'Malley (bass guitar, backing vocals), and Matt Helders (drums, backing vocals). They gained prominence with their debut album, "Whatever People Say I Am, That's What I'm Not," released in 2006.

ABOUT US

Arctic Monkeys originated in Sheffield, South Yorkshire, England. The band was formed in 2002 when its members were still teenagers. The founding members, Alex Turner, Jamie Cook, Matt Helders, and Andy Nicholson (old bassist), all attended Stocksbridge High School. They began playing music together and performing in local venues.



Their breakthrough came with the release of the single "I Bet You Look Good On The Dancefloor" in 2005, which went straight to number one on the UK Singles Chart. This success was followed by the release of their debut album, "Whatever People Say I Am, That's What I'm Not," in 2006, which also debuted at number one on the UK Albums Chart. The rapid rise to fame, combined with critical acclaim, solidified Arctic Monkeys as one of the most promising and successful bands of the mid-2000s.

MEMBERS

MATT HELDERS

Matthew Helders is an English drummer, vocalist and songwriter. He is best known as a founding member and the drummer of indie rock band Arctic Monkeys, with whom he has recorded seven studio albums.

JAMIE COOK

Jamie Cook is a guitarist and one of the founding members of the Arctic Monkeys. Born on July 8, 1985, in Sheffield, England, he has been an essential part of the band since its formation in 2002.

ALEX TURNER

Alex Turner is the lead vocalist, lyricist, and guitarist for the Arctic Monkeys. Born on January 6, 1986, in Sheffield, England, Turner is one of the primary songwriters for the band and is known for his distinctive voice and poetic lyrics.

NICK O'MALLEY

Nick O'Malley is the bassist for the Arctic Monkeys. Born on July 5, 1985, in Sheffield, England, O'Malley joined the band in 2006, replacing original bassist Andy Nicholson.
